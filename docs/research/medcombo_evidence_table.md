| Meyer et al., "How reliable are patient-completed medication reconciliation forms compared with pharmacy lists?" https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21855261/ | Observational comparison | Patient-completed medication forms can have omission and addition errors compared with pharmacy lists. | Medium | Intake should ask for source confidence and preserve uncertainty. |

| King et al., consumer-centered acetaminophen labeling https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21565649/ | Consumer labeling study | Consumers may not reliably recognize active ingredients in OTC acetaminophen products. | Medium | UI should surface active ingredients, not only product names. |
| RxNorm Overview https://www.nlm.nih.gov/research/umls/rxnorm/overview.html | Official data-source documentation | RxNorm normalizes prescription and many OTC drugs, but supplements and other categories are out of scope. | High | Use RxNorm for normalization, but preserve unknown and out-of-scope states. |
| DailyMed https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/ | Official labeling source | DailyMed can support source-linked product labeling. | High | Use for source references and labeling facts, not as a complete interaction engine. |
| openFDA Drug Labeling API https://open.fda.gov/apis/drug/label/ | Official API documentation | Labeling is available as machine-readable data, but openFDA warns against medical-care decisions and notes labeling limitations. | High | Use openFDA only behind source-freshness and limitation handling. |
| Van De Sijpe et al., DDI CDSS performance https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12911-022-01783-z | Quantitative evaluation and survey | Very severe DDI alerts had high override rates, with false positives tied to broad screening windows and missing patient-specific data. | High | Add patient-specific modifier fields and avoid alert-like certainty. |
| Felisberto et al., DDI alert override systematic review https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/14604582241263242 | Systematic review and meta-analysis | DDI alert overrides are common across CDSS settings. | High | Interaction UX should optimize specificity, context, and trust. |
| Abarca et al., DDI compendia concordance https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15098847/ | Compendia comparison | Major DDI severity ratings can disagree across compendia. | Medium | Severity labels require explicit source and rationale. |

## Evidence Gaps

- No MedCombo-specific clinical validation exists.
- No pharmacist-adjudicated MedCombo interaction set exists.
- No consumer comprehension study exists for MedCombo wording.
- Current benchmark coverage is intentionally small.
- Public-source DDI coverage remains incomplete and source-dependent.
